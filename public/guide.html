How to Improve Your Typing Speed

Whether you're just starting out or trying to break past a plateau, here's what actually works.

Complete Beginner? Start Here First

Before worrying about speed, you need to learn the correct finger placement. Typing fast with two fingers will always have a ceiling. Touch typing — where each finger has assigned keys — is the foundation.

We recommend starting at typing.com — it teaches you finger placement through structured lessons, starting from the home row (ASDF JKL;) and building up from there. Spend a week there before coming back to Typero.

The Core Principles

1. Accuracy before speed

Never sacrifice accuracy chasing WPM. Mistakes burn time and train bad habits. Use Strict mode in Typero — it forces you to fix every error before moving on.

2. Short, daily sessions beat long occasional ones

15–30 minutes a day beats a 3-hour session on the weekend. Muscle memory is built through repetition over time, not brute force. Use the 60s or 120s timer and do 5–10 runs daily.

3. Look at your results, not just the WPM

After each session Typero shows your worst characters. Those are the keys you should slow down on. Targeted practice on weak spots is more efficient than just typing more.

4. Don't look at the keyboard

If you peek, you'll always peek. Cover your hands if you have to. It feels slow at first — that's normal. Your fingers will learn where the keys are within days.

5. Track your streak

Log in to Typero and your activity heatmap will show daily consistency. Consistency is the single biggest predictor of long-term improvement.

6. Speed comes naturally

Once your accuracy is consistently above 95%, speed follows on its own. Focus on rhythm and smooth keystrokes — not hammering keys fast.
