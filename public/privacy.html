Privacy

Typero is a simple tool. This page explains exactly what is stored and what isn't.

You don't need an account

Typero works fully without logging in. Guest sessions are not linked to any identity. No IP, no browser fingerprint, nothing. Once the session ends, only the aggregate stats (WPM, accuracy) are stored — not what you typed.

If you create an account

We store:

  • Your chosen username (no email required)
  • A bcrypt hash of your password — the actual password is never stored
  • Your typing session stats: WPM, accuracy, duration, character counts, timestamp

We do not store:

  • The actual text you typed
  • Your email address
  • Your IP address
  • Any tracking or analytics data
  • Anything sold or shared with third parties

Authentication

Login uses a secure HTTP-only cookie with a JWT token. It expires after 30 days. No third-party auth providers, no OAuth, no social login. Your session lives only on this server and in your browser cookie.

Data storage

All data is stored securely on our server, hosted on AWS. There is no cloud sync, no external database service, and no data leaving the server this app runs on.

Deleting your data

There is currently no self-serve account deletion UI. If you'd like your account and all associated data removed, contact at dtg@soluto.in and it will be done.
